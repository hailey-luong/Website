DiscoverGaming

Overview

An app concept aimed to introduce seniors to gaming, our team designed and prototyped DiscoverGaming, which placed as a top ten finalist in the Adobe + Activision Creative Jam.

Each teammate began by leading one major section—my main focus being the gamified activities, but we all worked together as a team to finalize each section. We collaborated to develop the app concept, create a cohesive brand, arrange the page layouts, and prototype the experience.

Type

Challenge

We were tasked with developing an app experience for exploring video games as an avenue to connect with friends and family. Provided with the freedom to decide on a target audience, we chose to design an engaging and intuitive experience to introduce seniors to the world of video games

Solution

Our application presents a personalized selection of games based on the user’s preferences and abilities, captured through a series of activities and multiple-choice questions. All game recommendations are multiplayer to encourage social interaction with friends and family. We believe gaming should be for everyone, and DiscoverGaming helps reach a largely underserved demographic in the industry.

Final Product

Scheduling

The application includes a simple event planning system that facilitates social connection with friends and family. Although difficult to achieve during the COVID-19 pandemic, interaction remains an integral component in anyone's life. This function makes it easier for people to compare schedules, organize remote gatherings, and set reminders for events.

Abilities Quiz

Skill-testing activities and multiple choice questions are presented to capture the user's experience with hand eye-coordination, reflexes, and other abilities relevant to video games. As this application is geared towards seniors, it's necessary to recognize that many have little experience with technology and video games. Without being overly difficult to learn, presenting options that suit the user's abilities can motivate the user to better their skills.

Preferences Survey

Users are inquired about their preferences and skills they are seeking to develop. Providing recommendations that align with the user's interests encourages them to become try a new game. Regardless of preferences and skills, our app strives to match every individual with the perfect game.

Game List

Based on the survey and quiz results, the app offers personalized recommendations. The general categories we included were Party Classics, Creative, Brain Training, and Hardcore. Within each section the user is matched with, they are presented with a carefully selected list of games—each one featuring a basic overview with compatible platforms. The user has the option to add it to their Game List for future viewing and comparison.

Design System

Colours

With the target demographic being seniors, this app was designed to prioritize legibility and accessibility. This was achieved by including bold, high-contrast colours.

Typography

We ensured that all the text was fairly large, and decided on Open Sans as the typeface. The simple type pairing with the big font size ensured that all copy was readable for users any age.

Components

Paired with the typography and colour styles, large and distinct components were included throughout the app. Clickable buttons make use of drop shadows to imply affordance and rounded corners create a more friendly experience.

What I Learned

Time Management

As a 48-hour challenge, our team was limited to an extremely tight timeline. To develop a rough schedule, we deconstructed the process into various segments per day—the first for idea generation and producing branding components, the second for designing and prototyping, and the third for transitions and final revisions. The initial planning allowed us to solidify our direction and prioritize our tasks.

Simplicity

With consideration to our demographic of seniors, we produced branding elements and interactions that could be easily understood. We realized that we did not have to sacrifice visual appeal to produce an experience suitable for any user.

Team Work

Although we were each in charge of one portion, we continuously checked in with the other teammates and offered assistance as needed. Our open communication allowed us to highlight the strengths of each member and maintain a cohesive visual experience. The prototype development was a flexible process that adapted as it moved forward.
